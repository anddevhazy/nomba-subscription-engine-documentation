<p>Email is the one channel that fires on every recovery attempt, no exceptions. WhatsApp depends on a subscriber having WhatsApp; SMS depends on a phone number that accepts text; USSD depends on the subscriber dialling in themselves. Email just needs the address a subscriber signed up with, which every subscriber has, because it's how checkout confirmations and receipts already reach them.</p>

<h2 id="h-what">What arrives by email</h2>
<p>The same failed-charge notice as WhatsApp, formatted for a longer read and a written record.</p>
<blockquote>Subject: Your Lumen Monthly payment didn't go through<br />Your card was charged ₦15,000 for Lumen Monthly and the payment failed. [Retry Now] [Pause Subscription] [Downgrade Plan]</blockquote>
<p>Each action is a signed, single-use link rather than a button that needs an app to render, so the email works the same whether it's opened on a phone, a shared cybercafé desktop, or a work laptop.</p>

<h2 id="h-role">Why it's the baseline, not a fallback</h2>
<p>WhatsApp and SMS fall back to each other because a subscriber can only be reached on one of them at a time. Email isn't part of that either/or, it's sent in parallel on every recovery attempt, regardless of whether WhatsApp or SMS also succeed. That's deliberate: some merchants' subscribers are more email-native than phone-native (a B2B SaaS customer expensing a subscription, for instance), and a written, addressable record of every failed charge and every recovery is useful even when a faster channel already resolved it.</p>

<h2 id="h-scope">What email is, and isn't, used for</h2>
<p>Like WhatsApp, this channel is scoped to dunning, not general product communication. Plan changes a merchant makes, receipts, and marketing don't run through this pipeline; the recovery-orchestration component only sends what a subscriber needs to fix a specific failed charge.</p>

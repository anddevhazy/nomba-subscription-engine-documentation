<p>A subscriber can cancel from the portal the moment you turn nothing off, it's on by default. What you can add on top is a reason prompt and a last offer, both optional, both configured from <a href="/merchants/customer-portal/configure">Configure the customer portal</a>.</p>

<h2 id="h-reason">Collect a cancellation reason</h2>
<p>Turn on Cancellation reason and a subscriber picks one before their cancellation completes:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Too expensive</li>
  <li>Missing a feature I need</li>
  <li>Found a better alternative</li>
  <li>No longer need it</li>
  <li>Switching to a different plan</li>
  <li>Other, with a short free-text field</li>
</ul>
<div className="callout note"><div className="ic">ⓘ</div><p>This isn't a requirement in the platform's original spec, it's a small, low-risk addition: the reason is stored as metadata on the cancellation event, not a new entity in the domain model. If it's not useful to you, leave the toggle off.</p></div>

<h2 id="h-find">Finding the reasons your subscribers give</h2>
<p>Two places, both already covered elsewhere in these docs:</p>
<table>
  <tr><th>Where</th><th>What you see</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Merchant dashboard</td><td>The subscription's detail page shows the reason alongside its cancellation date.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>`subscription.cancelled` webhook</td><td>The event payload carries a `cancellationReason` field when reason capture is on. See <a href="/api-reference/webhook-events">Webhook events</a>.</td></tr>
</table>

<h2 id="h-offer">Offer Pause or Downgrade first</h2>
<p>Turn this on and a subscriber who taps Cancel sees two alternatives before the cancellation goes through: Pause, if the timing's wrong, or Downgrade, if the price is the actual problem. Same two actions already covered in <a href="/subscribers/manage-your-subscription">Manage your subscription</a> and in every dunning message a failed charge triggers, nothing new to build, just offered a step earlier.</p>
<p>There's no coupon or discount involved. This platform has no coupon entity, no discount code, nothing that changes a plan's price for one subscriber. If the reason for cancelling is price, the honest answer is a cheaper plan, not a temporary markdown on the current one.</p>

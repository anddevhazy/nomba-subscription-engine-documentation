<p>Look back at <a href="/channels/email">Email</a> or <a href="/channels/whatsapp">WhatsApp</a> and you'll notice something: Retry Now, Pause Subscription, and Downgrade Plan were never buttons wired to the general portal. Each one is a signed, single-use link scoped to exactly that action, for exactly that subscriber, expiring after a short window. This page formalizes that primitive as something you can generate yourself, for anything, not just a failed charge.</p>

<h2 id="h-concept">What a flow actually is</h2>
<p>A flow is a portal link scoped to one action instead of the whole portal. Opening it drops a subscriber straight into that single screen, with the rest of the portal's navigation hidden, so there's nothing to get lost in. You can set what happens when they finish: redirect back to your own site, or show a plain confirmation and stop.</p>

<h2 id="h-types">Flow types</h2>
<table>
  <tr><th>Type</th><th>What it does</th></tr>
  <tr><td>`update_payment_method`</td><td>Land the subscriber straight on the card-update screen. The exact link our dunning messages send on a failed charge.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>`pause_subscription`</td><td>One tap pauses, no navigation required.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>`resume_subscription`</td><td>For a subscriber who paused and is ready to come back.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>`cancel_subscription`</td><td>Opens straight to cancellation, including the reason prompt and Pause/Downgrade offer if you've enabled them.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>`change_plan`</td><td>Opens the plan-switch screen, respecting whatever plan restrictions you've configured.</td></tr>
</table>

<h2 id="h-create">Creating a flow</h2>
<p>Same endpoint as a general portal link, with an `action` specified:</p>
<pre><code>{`curl -X POST https://api.nomba-subscriptions.com/portal/links \\
  -H "Authorization: Bearer nse_live_..." \\
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \\
  -d '{
    "customerId": "cus_01HMFD...",
    "action": "update_payment_method",
    "subscriptionId": "sub_01HMFC...",
    "redirectUrl": "https://efe-ledgerly.example.com/billing/updated"
  }'`}</code></pre>
<p>Response is the same shape as a general link:</p>
<pre><code>{`{
  "url": "https://portal.nomba-subscriptions.com/l/plnk_01HMFF...",
  "expiresAt": "2026-08-14T11:17:00Z"
}`}</code></pre>
<p>Use this from your own support tooling, "here's a link to update your card" is a much better reply to a support ticket than a password-reset-style explanation of how to log in.</p>

<h2 id="h-after">Customizing what happens after</h2>
<p>Set `redirectUrl` and a subscriber lands back on your own site the moment they finish the action, useful when the flow is embedded inside your own product's settings page and you don't want them stranded on our domain. Leave it out and they see a plain confirmation screen instead, with a link back to the full portal if they want to look around.</p>
